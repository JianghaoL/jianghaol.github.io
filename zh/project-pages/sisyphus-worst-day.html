Overview

Sisyphus's Worst Day is a USC Advanced Game Project that has a development period of 10 months. It is a third-person puzzle game that reimagines the myth of Sisyphus in a humorous and modern context. Players take on the role of an office worker, led by the gods, who must trap Sisyphus in an endless loop of mundane tasks.

As the Lead Technical Sound Designer, I was responsible for overseeing the audio production process, ensuring that all sound elements were effectively integrated into the game using FMOD and Unity. I collaborated closely with a team of five sound designers to create an immersive audio experience that complemented the game's unique narrative and gameplay mechanics.

I worked intensively on audio implementation, utilizing C# scripting within Unity to ensure seamless integration of sound effects and music. This involved setting up interactive audio systems that responded dynamically to player actions and game events, enhancing the overall player experience.

Throughout the development process, I coordinated with other departments to ensure that the audio design aligned with the game's visual style and storytelling. I provided technical support and guidance to the sound design team, helping them to optimize their workflows and achieve high-quality results. Having close collaboration with the design team, we ensured that the audio elements effectively conveyed the game's humor and themes. The result is a cohesive and engaging puzzle game that offers players a fresh take on the classic myth of Sisyphus.
